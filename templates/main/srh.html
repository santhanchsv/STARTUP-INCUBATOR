How to file for a patent

Introduction

A Patent is a statutory right for an invention granted for a limited period of time to the patentee by the Government, in exchange for full disclosure of his invention for excluding others from making, using, selling, importing the patented product or process for producing that product for those purposes without his consent.

An invention is patentable if it meets the following criteria:

  • It should be novel.
  • It should have an inventive step or be non-obvious.
  • It should be capable of Industrial application.
  • It should not fall under the categories of inventions that are not patentable under sections 3 and 4 of the Act.

Types of patent application that can be filed

  • Provisional Application: Filed when the invention is still under experimentation stage.
  • Ordinary Application: Filed in the Patent Office without claiming any priority.
  • Convention Application: Filed claiming a priority date based on the same or substantially similar application filed in one or more convention countries.
  • PCT International Application: Filed under the Patent Cooperation Treaty in more than 150 countries by a single application.
  • PCT National Phase Application: Filed in India within 31 months from the international filing date or the priority date, whichever is earlier.
  • Patent of Addition: Filed for a slight modification of an earlier invention for which a patent has already been applied for or obtained.
  • Divisional Application: Filed when an application claims more than one invention.

Applying for patent

A patent application can be filed with the Indian Patent Office either with the provisional specification or with complete specification along with the prescribed fee. Forms required for filing a patent application include Form 1 (request for filing an application), Form 2 (provisional or complete specification), Form 5 (declaration of inventorship), Form 18 (request for examination), and more.

Searching the database for already filed patents

Free searches can be performed on the Indian Patent database available on the Patent Office website. The website also contains innovative tools under DYNAMIC UTILITIES which give information about the patent applications at various stages of processing.

Schemes available for MSME's and Startups with respect to IPRs

The Government of India has formulated schemes to spur the innovative capabilities of MSME‘s and Start-ups. MSMEs are provided with a 50% fee concession on patent filing fees, while Start-ups are offered a rebate of 80% on patent fees. Additionally, expedited examination and reimbursement of facilitator expenditure are provided for Start-ups.

Forms required for filing a patent application:

Generally, in order to file an application, an applicant is required to file:

  • Form 1: Application for grant of a patent.
  • Form 2: Contains invention including Title, Field, background, prior act, drawbacks of the known inventions, description, experimental results, drawings, Abstract of the Invention, etc.
  • Form 5: Declaration of inventorship.
  • Form 18: Request for examination of a patent.
  • Form 18A: Request for expedited examination of a patent application (if the applicant is a startup).
  • Form 26: The form of authorization of Patent Agent.
  • Form 9: Request for early publication.

Important timelines:

  • Form 2 to be filed within 12 months of filing the provisional specification.
  • Form 18 to be filed within 48 months of filing application or priority.
  • Form 5 to be filled within 1 month from the date of filing of the complete specifications.

Searching the database for already filed patents:

The person concerned can perform a search free of cost on Indian Patent database consisting of published patent applications and granted patents. The said database is available on the Patent Office website as inPASS here. Further, the website www.ipindia.nic.in contains innovative tools under DYNAMIC UTILITIES which give information about the patent applications at various stages of processing.

Schemes available for MSME's and Startups with respect to IPRs:

The Government of India has formulated schemes to spur the innovative capabilities of the innovators who are from MSME‘s and Start-ups.

MSME’s: A 50% fee concession is provided for MSMEs vis-à-vis large entities on patent filing fees.

A Scheme for facilitating Startups Intellectual Property Protection (SIPP): This scheme has been launched for encouraging innovation and creativity of Startups. Startups are provided a rebate of 80% on patent fees as compared to the fee paid by a legal entity.

For effective implementation of the scheme, facilitators have been impaneled by the Controller General of Patent, Trademark, and Design (CGPDTM). Presently around 423 facilitators in Patent & Designs, 606 Facilitators in Trademarks have been empaneled under this scheme.

The government of India provides the following benefits to Start-Ups in filing the patent application in India under Scheme for Facilitating Start-ups Intellectual Property Protection (SIPP):

  • An entity qualifying as a 'startup‘ under Stand-Up India‖ initiative of the Government of India can avail the facility of expedited examination.
  • The Government reimburses the expenditure to the facilitator who assists the startup for filing and prosecuting the patent application to the extent of Rs.10,000/-
